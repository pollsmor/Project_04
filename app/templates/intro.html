Epidemics Compared: Day by Day

By ihatequarantine (Derek Leung, Kevin Li, Justin Shaw, and Albert Wan)

Our webpage compares various epidemics from recent years. It compares them day by day from the beginning of each. The X-axis shows the number of elapsed days and the Y-axis shows the total amount of deaths caused by each.

A lot of insightful data can be deciphered from our graph; for example, one can easily see how COVID-19 took the world by storm, while the MERS epidemic is slothful enough to make you question whether it should even be classified as one.

Note: it is highly recommended to zoom out on your browser a few times to fit everything on screen. On Windows, the hotkeys would be Ctrl + Plus. On macOS, it would be Command + Plus. You can go back to default zoom by substituting "0" for "+" afterwards.
